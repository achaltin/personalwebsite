Campus Involvement

Kappa Phi Delta Sorority

January 2016 to Present

I pledged Kappa Phi Delta Sorority in the Spring of 2016. I joined to find a supportive group of women on campus, and that's exactly what I got. My first acitve semester, Fall 2016, I was the Public Relations Chair. I created advertisements for a wide variety of events and mediums, including recruitment events, philanthropy or fundraising events, and social events, for print and various social media platforms.

Download some of my PR projects


During 2017, I served as the Secretary. During my term, I focused on improving meeting effieiciency and inter-sorority relations. I reformatted our meeting minutes to prioritize the time of our sisters, and instituted a schedule to follow, allowing sisters to plan their time more accurately. I also began the Quality Kappa Awards to recognize sisters who portrayed our qualities and values well.

For 2018, I am the President of Kappa Phi Delta Sorority. My main goals are policy updates, including but not limited to the Bylaws, Standing Rules, Risk Management Policy, Scholarship Policy, and Membership Policy. As secretary, I noticed a disconnect from our policies and our values, and I seek to align thoses. I also aim to better our campus relations. I have started inviting IIT Faculty to meals at the Kappa Phi Delta house in order for us to get to know them, and to let them see who we are. I hope to improve the perception not only of Kappa, but of Greek Life overall at IIT.


Our executive board terms are year long, starting in January and running from the Srping semester to the fall. This is to ease the turnover process and prevent drastic information loss when previous executive board members graduate in the Spring.

View a sample of my meeting minutes format. Only the Business table is discussed out loud, anything in Dates/Announcements is read silently at the beginning of the meeting. I limit Business Discussion to last only until 10:30pm for the health of our sisters.

Order of Omega

January 2017 to Present

Order of Omega is a Greek Honor's Society. Members are invited to apply if they are in at least their third year, and have a GPA in the top 3% of the Greek Community. They are then selected based on campus involvement and philanthropy, and only 15 new members can join each year. I was initiated into the Tau Eta Chapter of Order of Omega my second year, as I had enough credits to be a third year at that time.

I really value the opportunites for service and philanthropy with Order of Omega. We do at least one major service event each semester, usually in the Bronzeville community. I feel it's incredibly important to give back to the surrounding community, as opposed to distancing from it. I also enjoy the opportunity to get to engage with other like minded Greeks from the campus community.
